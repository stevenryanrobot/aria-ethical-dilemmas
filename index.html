As humanoid robots become technically mature enough to enter the home, the question is no longer only what they can do. It is what happens when we configure them to optimize some values over others.

Ethical Question

When a humanoid home robot is configured to improve safety, convenience, obedience, or emotional support, can it raise one person's quality of life while reducing another person's dignity, trust, privacy, or autonomy? If so, how should we design and constrain such a system?

What you do

You configure the robot's default priorities before each week and then observe what those settings produce inside the household.

What carries forward

Rosa's dignity, family trust, privacy, Leo's social development, and cultural belonging persist across weeks.

What this shows

A home robot can look useful on the surface while quietly shifting power, dependence, and fairness inside the family.
